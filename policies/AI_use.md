# AI Use Policy

## Core Requirements
We don't prohibit using AI for coding, writing, and other tasks, but we require
that you use it responsibly.

1. **You must be able to explain everything in your project**
   - Every single line of code, every single comment, every design decision
   - If you cannot do that, you are using AI improperly

2. **Results produced with AI should be of higher quality than results without
   it**
   - AI is a tool to enhance your work, not replace your critical thinking

3. **Be careful about becoming dependent on AI**
   - If without AI you cannot work (e.g., write code, think through problems,
     debug issues), that's a red flag
   - Use AI to augment your capabilities, not to bypass learning or
     problem-solving

4. **Use AI for brainstorming, but exercise critical judgment**
   - Always verify AI suggestions for accuracy
   - Protect against hallucinations and don't let the machine take over your
     decision-making
   - Challenge outputs and make informed choices

## Quality Standards: Avoid AI Artifacts
- Humans are put off by content that looks AI-generated. Avoid these obvious
  signs:
  - **Excessive em-dashes** throughout text
  - **Page separators** (horizontal rules like `---`)
  - **Excessive markdown formatting** (overuse of bold, italic, or other emphasis)
  - **Obvious AI-generated prose** with unnaturally formal or generic language

- Use tools like `/text.humanize` to refine output and make it sound more natural
  and authentic (I know it sounds contradictory and hypocritical)

## Accountability
- Your peers will review your work
- Be prepared to justify your use of AI and demonstrate that you understand and
  can defend every component of what you've created.
